BedJet's Design Tradeoffs, Solved by Perfectly Snug

Cooling the bed is the goal. The question is whether the product gets out of the way while doing it. Here's where BedJet's approach adds friction and how Perfectly Snug gets around it.

Clutter: hose, unit, floor space
BedJet 3

Control unit sits on the floor at the foot of your bed. A hose runs up under your sheets. Some setups use two.

Perfectly Snug

Fans are built into the topper itself. Nothing on the floor, no hose, no extra furniture in the bedroom.

Cools the air, not the mattress
BedJet 3

Pushes air through the hose into the space between your sheets. The mattress underneath stays warm, which is the actual source of most overnight heat buildup.

Perfectly Snug

Pulls heat and moisture out from below your body through the topper's 3D airflow structure. The heat source gets addressed directly.

Uneven airflow, hot and cold pockets
BedJet 3

Air enters from one end of the bed. Coverage can feel strong at the feet, weaker at the chest and back.

Perfectly Snug

Airflow is distributed evenly across the topper, so cooling reaches your whole body instead of one zone.

Remote control only
BedJet 3

Adjustments happen through a remote. If it's off the nightstand at 3 AM, you're waking up to find it.

Perfectly Snug

Buttons built into each side of the bed. Reach down, tap, back to sleep. App control is optional, not required.

No body temperature sensing
BedJet 3

Set it, and it runs at that setting. It doesn't know if you've cooled off, warmed up, or kicked the covers off.

Perfectly Snug

Built-in sensors track your skin temperature and adjust the airflow thousands of times per night. You stay in range without touching anything.

Single-zone on a shared bed
BedJet 3

One setting for the whole bed. Dual-zone requires a second full unit, which means double the clutter.

Perfectly Snug

Dual-zone is built in. Each side of the bed controls independently, with one unit.

Noise near your head
BedJet 3

The unit sits within a few feet of where you sleep, and the hose carries fan noise right into the sheets.

Perfectly Snug

Fans sit at the foot of the topper, away from your head. A quiet mode in the app caps the fan speed if you're a light sleeper.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the Smart Topper cost more than a BedJet?

BedJet is a fan in a box with a hose. The Smart Topper is a mattress topper with built-in sensors, dual-zone control, automatic temperature adjustment, moisture management, and a foot heater, all wired into the bed itself. The price difference reflects a different product.

If a fan at the foot of the bed is what you need, BedJet is cheaper and will do that job. If you want the cooling to work without you managing it, the Smart Topper is doing more work.

What size should I order?

Perfectly Snug fits standard mattress sizes: Twin, Twin XL, Full, Queen, King, California King, and Split King. Check the size guide on the product page for exact dimensions.

Does it work on an articulated bed?

Yes. We recommend using the included bed-corner straps to keep the Smart Topper in place on an adjustable base.

How is it powered?

Low voltage DC power from a power brick that plugs into any standard wall outlet. The power supply works in any country.

Can I wash the Smart Topper?

The cover is removable and machine washable. The cooling layer underneath is not washed directly.

How quiet is it?

At low cooling levels, the Smart Topper is nearly silent. At 50% cooling, it's similar to the sound of household forced-air heating. At full cooling, it's about as loud as a stand-alone fan running in the room. If you're sensitive to sound, the app has a quiet mode that caps the fans at 50%.

Is it comfortable?

The Smart Topper is designed to be thin enough to preserve the feel of your existing mattress. On particularly soft beds, you'll notice a slight firming effect.

Does it work in a hot room?

Yes. At room temperatures of 80°F, Perfectly Snug had 102% more cooling power than the top water-based competitor. Effective up to about 82°F / 28°C room temperature.

Where is it made?

Designed and made by Perfectly Snug in British Columbia, Canada. Third-party safety certified and compliant with North American safety standards.
